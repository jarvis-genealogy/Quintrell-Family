Heartland

       

On the edge of the Nullarbor

Where the sun shimmers on an empty plain

I see an eagle rise, wings spread wide on a weightless wind,

And my heart soars with its flight

Into a sky that is vast and blue and beyond my imagining

 

On the cliffs at the Head of Bight

Where the sea breaks onto timeless rocks

I see a whale rise, breaching wide to the circling sea

And my heart vaults with its cry

Over a sea that is vast and blue and beyond my believing

 

Our hearts rise wide to the open sky

From sea to desert our spirits fly

On the winds of our destiny

To our heartland

To our heartland.

 

At the base of strong Uluru

Where a rock anchors a nation’s heart

I see a people rise, spirits wide to an ageless sky

And my heart leaps with their hope

Into a day that is vast and new and beyond my dreaming

 

Bridge:

And our hearts rise, and our hearts rise

 

Our hearts rise wide to the open sky

From sea to desert our spirits fly

On the winds of our destiny

To our heartland

To our heartland.
