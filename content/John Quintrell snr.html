JOHN QUINTRELL

b. 20 Jun 1817, Breage, Cornwall d. 10 Feb 1888, Moonta Mines SA

m. Mary Toy 9 Sep 1837 Breage, Cornwall

b. 1819 Breage, Cornwall d. 23 Aug 1894 Moonta SA

John and Mary departed London on 12 April 1858 (other records suggest Plymouth on 17 May 1858 or Plymouth on 12 April 1858) on the ‘General Hewett’, (1) arriving at Port Adelaide SA on 11 Sep 1858. John’s occupation is shown as ‘miner’. They were accompanied by all of their children.

(1) The 'General Hewitt' was a three-masted fully rigged ship of 973 tonnes built in Bengal in 1812 because of the availability of good mahogany. It was used for convict transportation to New South Wales, and possibly to Van Dieman's Land (Tasmania) from 1814. It had a crew of around 80. It made various journeys to Sydney via Port Adelaide and Port Phillip Bay in the 1850s.

(2) John was previously married but no records could be identified of his first marriage.

*John Quintrell and Mary Toy are Neil's great-great-grandparents.
